Nature at Tregulland

 

In the woodland by Tregulland's bridge over the River Inny, mixed flocks of tits can be seen flitting through the trees, while dippers and kingfishers zip up and down the river and the plaintive call of bullfinches can be heard from the hedgerows. Last week, we had one Canada goose back on the lake who disturbed a woodcock from some wet grassland nearby. Goldcrests are often heard, and occasionally seen in the conifers beside the lake. Tawny owls were calling (during the day!) last week, causing alarm for the local blackbirds.

Check our nature page for more info or get updates by following us on Twitter.
