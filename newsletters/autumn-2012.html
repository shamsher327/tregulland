Nature at Tregulland

 

The kestrels have now reappeared in the valley as they did this time last year, and were often seen through last winter hunting for small mammals over the rough grassland. We are hoping they find the nest box we put up for them in the valley bottom.

The starlings, that return to winter in the area, have not yet appeared but we can expect the massive murmurations to be arriving soon, flying over Tregulland in their millions to roost in the woods surrounding nearby Crowdy Reservoir and Rough Tor.

The otters, that move up and down the River Inny and past the lake during the night, still remain largely unseen but leave their tell tale "calling cards" in the form of spraints on many prominent stones in the river and along the bank. This enables us to tell when they have been moving through the grounds of Tregulland.

Check our nature page for more info or get updates by following us on Twitter.
